About Me

Welcome to my Geographic Information Systems (GIS) Portfolio! My name is Evelyn Gorey, and I recently graduated from Temple University's Geography & Urban Studies undergraduate program (B.A.) with an undergraduate GIS certificate.

I currently work as an urban forestry GIS intern for the Pennsylvania Horticultural Society (PHS) and Philadelphia Parks & Rec; in this role, I conduct an urban tree canopy prioritization analysis for the City of Philadelphia to aid strategies developed under the Philadelphia Urban Forest Strategic Plan. I also work as a remote sensing research assistant at Temple University, where I use remote sensing techniques to analyze land cover changes and key drivers of biodiversity loss in northern Colombia.

Some of my past roles include the GIS Summer of Maps Fellow at Azavea, the GIS Analyst Intern at Temple University's Facilities Information Resources & Management (FIRM) office, and the GIS Research Assistant at Temple University Ambler Campus.

Through my academic research and prior work experience, I've developed a passion for using GIS to inform environmental conservation and city planning decisions, to convey spatial information to various audiences in engaging ways, and to encourage everyone to participate in mapping efforts.
